About Daman Municipal Council

A Historical and
Administrative Overview

The Daman Municipal Council, established during Portuguese rule in the 16th century, is one of the oldest municipalities in Asia. Governed by the Daman and Diu Municipalities (Amended) Regulation, 1994, this statutory body oversees the administration of Daman, the capital of the Union Territory of Daman and Diu.



The Daman Municipal Council, one of Asia's oldest municipalities, has its roots in the Portuguese era of the 16th century. Established as "CAMARA DE DAMAO," it functioned under "Senado Publico Nobre e Leal Proviso De 1581" until 1969. Post-liberation, the Council was restructured under the 1968 Municipalities Act, with the first elections held in 1970. The Council, led by elected President and Vice President, focuses on obligatory functions like road and public facility maintenance, and discretionary functions like park and library development. Key officials from government departments support the Council's operations, ensuring the well-being of Daman's residents.

HISTORY OF COUNCIL

It was built in the 16th century during the rule of the Portuguese, D.C.-Da-Braganasa, by the order of Portuguese hero who won the suppression, and it was granted the status of municipality of the city of Evora in 1581 in Portugal. This Evora municipality is now a memorial included in UNESCO World Heritage in Portugal.


It was fully operational in 1585. This municipality is the oldest municipal council in Asia. At the time of its construction, the engineering and extension of the Portuguese was on the brightness and there was a very fast work in the thick Daman fort.
